I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Colorado studying computer security. My research interests include machine learning, peer-to-peer networks, and distributed ledger technologies. I am currently working on improving the security and scalability of blockchain networks.

Research Focus

My research aims to address some of the critical challenges in the fields of security and data communication over decentralized networks. Specifically, I am focused on developing advanced methods to enhance the security frameworks of blockchain technologies and peer-to-peer systems.


Machine Learning Applications in Security

I explore the application of machine learning techniques to predict and mitigate security vulnerabilities in networked systems. My work includes using AI to automate threat detection and response in real-time, enhancing the resilience of distributed networks against malicious attacks.


Peer-to-Peer Networks & Distributed Ledger Technologies

My interest in peer-to-peer networks and distributed ledger technologies revolves around improving transaction efficiency and security. This includes optimizing blockchain protocols for faster block propagation times and reduced latency, and studying the implications of network topology on blockchain performance.
